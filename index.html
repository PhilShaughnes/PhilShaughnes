Localgivr

Micro-philanthropy without the friction.

Localgivr makes it simple to find vetted, meaningful, local needs you can help with right now.

As my (and my partners) final project, this app is one of my favorites. Mostly, it solves a real need and helps people help each other. It solves the problem of visible ways to help being either:

(1) Hard to make a significant impact without losing your shirt (e.g. - cancer fundraiser) or

(2) Not necessarily helpful, and possibly harmful (e.g. - giving the guy on the street money).

Vetted non-profits submit needs, which are then matched to users based on location, type of need, and what categories and organizations that user has selected. Each user recieves a weekly text notification for a matched need. Following the link takes you a portal provided by the organization where you can instantly sign up to volunteer or purchase supplies straight away!

This was a joint final project for The Iron Yard. It is built on Ruby-on-Rails, utilizing React and bootstrap on the front-end. See the live demo here
